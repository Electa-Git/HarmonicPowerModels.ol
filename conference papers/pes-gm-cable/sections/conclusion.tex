\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

This paper presents a holistic one-shot optimization method to determine the harmonic hosting capacity of a power system. The presented approach better utilizes the power system compared to the standards IEEE519-2022 and IEC61000-3-6:2008 which tend to over- or underestimate the harmonic hosting capacity, respectively. More importantly the presented method does not lead to any harmonic voltage limit violations, which the existing standards miss to capture near resonances. In power systems with increasing share of underground cable connections, the shift in the resonance frequency amplifies this effect. In this work, the obtained harmonic hosting capacity equally distributes the available harmonic budget among all units whereas future work will investigate different fairness criteria for better utilizing the harmonic hosting capacity of a given power system, without compromising on equity and fairness between different units.
